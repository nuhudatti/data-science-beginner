Section 4

Statistical Operations on Arrays

Master essential statistical functions to analyze and derive insights from your data using NumPy's powerful computation capabilities.

Core Statistical Functions

Measures of Central Tendency

  • np.mean() - Arithmetic average
  • np.median() - Middle value
  • np.average() - Weighted average

Measures of Dispersion

  • np.std() - Standard deviation
  • np.var() - Variance
  • np.ptp() - Range (max - min)

Aggregation Functions

  • np.sum() - Sum of elements
  • np.min() - Minimum value
  • np.max() - Maximum value

Distribution Functions

  • np.percentile() - Value at a percentile
  • np.quantile() - Value at a quantile
  • np.histogram() - Frequency distribution

Why Statistical Operations Matter

Statistical operations transform raw data into meaningful insights. They allow you to:

  • Understand data distribution and spread
  • Identify trends and patterns
  • Detect outliers and anomalies
  • Make data-driven decisions
  • Prepare data for machine learning

Practical Examples

1. Basic Statistical Analysis

basic_stats.py 
1import numpy as np
2
3# Sample data: Crop yields (tons/hectare)
4yields = np.array([5.2, 4.9, 6.0, 5.5, 4.8, 6.5, 5.7, 5.9])
5
6# Basic statistics
7mean_yield = np.mean(yields)      # Average yield
8median_yield = np.median(yields)  # Middle value
9std_dev = np.std(yields)         # Standard deviation
10min_yield = np.min(yields)        # Minimum yield
11max_yield = np.max(yields)        # Maximum yield
12
13# Percentiles
14q1 = np.percentile(yields, 25)  # First quartile
15q3 = np.percentile(yields, 75)  # Third quartile
16
17# Print results
18print(f"Average Yield: {mean_yield:.2f} tons/ha")
19print(f"Standard Deviation: {std_dev:.2f}")
20print(f"Yield Range: {min_yield} to {max_yield}")
21print(f"Interquartile Range: {q3 - q1:.2f}")

Advanced Statistical Analysis

Correlation and Covariance

Measure relationships between different datasets.

correlation.py 
1# Rainfall (mm) and crop yields (tons/ha)
2rainfall = np.array([120, 150, 180, 90, 130, 160, 110, 140])
3yields = np.array([5.2, 5.8, 6.2, 4.9, 5.5, 6.0, 5.0, 5.7])
4
5# Calculate covariance matrix
6cov_matrix = np.cov(rainfall, yields)
7
8# Calculate correlation coefficient
9correlation = np.corrcoef(rainfall, yields)[0, 1]
10
11print(f"Covariance Matrix:\n", cov_matrix)
12print(f"\nCorrelation Coefficient: {correlation:.2f}")
Understanding Correlation

Correlation measures the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables:

  • +1.0: Perfect positive correlation
  • 0.0: No correlation
  • -1.0: Perfect negative correlation
  • 0.7-1.0: Strong positive correlation
  • 0.3-0.7: Moderate correlation
  • 0.0-0.3: Weak correlation

Real-World Application: Agricultural Decision Making

Statistical analysis helps farmers make data-driven decisions about crop selection, resource allocation, and yield optimization.

farm_decision.py 
1import numpy as np
2
3# Historical data: [rainfall, temperature, yield]
4data = np.array([
5    [120, 28.5, 5.2],  # Year 1
6    [150, 29.0, 5.8],  # Year 2
7    [180, 27.8, 6.2],  # Year 3
8    [90, 30.2, 4.9],   # Year 4
9    [130, 28.9, 5.5],  # Year 5
10    [160, 27.5, 6.0],  # Year 6
11    [110, 29.5, 5.0],  # Year 7
12    [140, 28.2, 5.7]   # Year 8
13])
14
15# Calculate correlations
16corr_matrix = np.corrcoef(data, rowvar=False)
17
18# Extract key correlations
19rain_yield_corr = corr_matrix[0, 2]   # Rainfall vs Yield
20temp_yield_corr = corr_matrix[1, 2]   # Temperature vs Yield
21
22# Statistical summary for decision making
23optimal_rain = np.percentile(data[:, 0], 75)  # 75th percentile rainfall
24optimal_temp = np.median(data[:, 1])        # Median temperature
25
26print(f"Rainfall-Yield Correlation: {rain_yield_corr:.2f}")
27print(f"Temperature-Yield Correlation: {temp_yield_corr:.2f}")
28print(f"Optimal Rainfall: {optimal_rain} mm")
29print(f"Optimal Temperature: {optimal_temp}°C")
