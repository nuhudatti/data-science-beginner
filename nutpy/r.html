Agricultural Yield Analysis

Apply all your NumPy skills to analyze real agricultural data, derive insights, and make data-driven decisions for improved farming outcomes.

Project Overview: Nigerian Farm Data Analysis

The Dataset

  • Crop yields from 16 farm plots across Nigeria
  • 4 different crop types (Maize, Rice, Sorghum, Cassava)
  • 3 growing seasons (2020-2022)
  • Additional data: Rainfall, Temperature, Soil Quality
  • Total records: 144 data points

Analysis Goals

  • Identify highest and lowest performing crops
  • Analyze yield trends over seasons
  • Determine optimal growing conditions
  • Find correlations between environmental factors and yield
  • Recommend improvements for future seasons

Why This Matters for Nigeria

Agriculture contributes 22% to Nigeria's GDP and employs over 36% of the workforce. Data-driven agriculture can:

  • Increase crop yields by 20-30%
  • Reduce resource waste
  • Improve food security
  • Increase farmer incomes
  • Boost economic growth in rural areas

Insights & Recommendations

Key Findings

  • Sorghum had the highest average yield (6.2 tons/ha)
  • Rice showed the most sensitivity to rainfall variations
  • Optimal temperature range: 26-29°C for most crops
  • Soil quality has strong correlation with yield (r=0.78)
  • Top-performing farms had consistent soil quality >7

Recommendations

  • Expand sorghum cultivation in northern regions
  • Implement irrigation systems for rice farms
  • Add soil enrichment programs for low-quality plots
  • Develop temperature monitoring systems
  • Create crop rotation schedule based on soil data
